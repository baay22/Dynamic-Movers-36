Nightlife in the Upper West Side

Nightlife has changed drastically over the past several decades.

First, let’s start off with the obvious. People’s definition of nightlife has changed dramatically over the past several decades. If you’ve asked a New Yorker 20 years ago what he considered a good night out to be in the Big Apple… You probably would’ve gotten an answer in the vein of DJ driven clubs. However, today if you’ve asked the same person that same exact question… You would get a completely different answer! Today, nightlife is also late-night restaurants, cocktail bars, pubs, after-hours sports, and recreation.

Moreover, getting to places in the Upper West Side is easier than ever before. Long gone are the days of waiting for taxis and relying on designated drivers. Today we live in a society of Lyfts and Ubers. Where getting a ride from point A to point B is only a mere touch of a screen away.

The nightlife is dead, long live the nightlife!

With that being said, most people today can hardly recognize nightlife as it was known back in the day. Comedy clubs, after-hour pubs, and recreational facilities have given a breath of fresh air to New York’s after-hours entertainment. Which led to making an unapproachable New York… Well, rather welcoming.

This led to a massive influx of people in New York searching for the new leisurely activities this city has to offer. This, of course, gave rise to people hiring long distance movers NYC in order to relocate to the Big Apple. So, if you are new to New York, let us welcome you to the undiscovered world of Nightlife in the Upper West Side.

Smoke Jazz and Supper Club

Are you a jazzhead? Or, are you may be looking for something different in the nightlife scene of the Big Apple? Something refreshing? Well, if that’s the case then the Smoke Jazz and Supper Club Lounge, located near Harlem, is the place to be. The atmosphere that this club creates is something you need to experience at least once in your lifetime. Even if you don’t like jazz. The combination of live world-renown jazz music, candlelit tables, and velvet banquets, really set a mood for you to experience jazz in a way you never did before. And, on top of that, the food is served by the critically acclaimed chef Patricia Williams. And that’s why it’s definitely a must-have experience, even though the Smoke Jazz and Supper club might not be on anyone’s list of top rock bars in Manhattanv.

Dive bar NYC

We are sure you will never run out of options to drink or eat in the Dive bar NYC.

While on the topic of food, drinks, and music, we would be remiss if we didn’t mention the Dive Bar in New York. This lounge has a mind-boggling selection of on-tap beers, draft beer, and craft beers that is constantly being refreshed, refurbished, and modernized. The tastes of which, will keep you on your toes every time you decide to visit. On top of all that, if you somehow manage to quench your taste buds in all of that different flavored beer… There are high-quality scotch, bourbon, and tequila beverages waiting for you.

However, if all that alcohol makes you hungry, the Dive Bar NYC offers one of the best burgers and sandwiches in town, until the dead of night.

Although the fun doesn’t stop there. Every night there are trivia quizzes, live sport streams, and music being played. So, you can stop by and watch your favorite team play on one of a number of high-quality TV sets, or enjoy an awesome trivia game with friends. We are sure that you won’t run out of things to do once you visit the Dive bar in NYC. Even if you decide to visit it multiple times.

Nightlife in the Upper West Side – The Comedy Club

While stand-up comedy might not be the first thing that comes to mind when talking about nightlife, it has definitely become quite the giant in the late-night entertainment domain.

There are a number of clubs, restaurants, cocktail bars we could mention. However, none have a longstanding tradition as The Comedy Club. Since 1986 this place has been the platform for comedy legends, such as Chris Rock, Jerry Seinfeld, and Louis CK. This humble abode is located in the residential block of the 78th Street of the Upper Westside. People have renovated this rather old, traditional-looking building to suit the demands of modern culture. The Comedy Club has a showroom that can fit 125 guests, awesome bar snacks, and even better comedians will definitely have you champing at the bits. It is the premier place to be if having a laugh, drinks and a fun time with your friends is what you are looking for.
